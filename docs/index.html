Summing Matrices

That I can remember, I've always liked numbers. The ways they behave and relate has always aroused my interest. While I was in college, there were always fun mathematical problems to solve running around the corridors and I was one, amongst a bunch of people, who got entertained by trying to solve them. At some point, this simple problem showed up:

Fill a 3 by 3 square with the numbers from 1 to 9 so that the sum of the numbers on every row, column and both diagonals are the same.

Afther a little trial and error, the solution is easy to achieve:

2 9 4
7 5 3
6 1 8

And the sum of the numbers on every row column and both diagonals is 15.

And, as usual, solving just the problem at hand isn't enough. I embarked in the taks of trying to solve the same problem for a 5 by 5 square. After a little bit more trial and error than the first time, the solution presented itself:

3 20 7 24 11
16 8 25 12 4
9 21 13 5 17
22 14 1 18 10
15 2 19 6 23

Where the sum of the numbers on every row column and both diagonals is 65.

At this point, I thought it must be likely that all odd-sided squares have a similar solution and, consequently, there must be a generic way to solve this problem for all. And, following that conviction, I started looking for patterns and some stand out with the "naked eye".

2 9 4
7 5 3
6 1 8
3 20 7 24 11
16 8 25 12 4
9 21 13 5 17
22 14 1 18 10
15 2 19 6 23
  • In the middle of the square goes medium (the middle nummber): 5 in 3x3; 13 in 5x5
  • Bellow medium number goes the first number, i.e., 1
  • Over medium number goes the last number: 9 in 3x3; 25 in 5x5
  • Top-right to bottom-left diagonal is in sequence, having the medium number, in the middle, which gives the most important data to work with, i.e., the sum value:
    • 4, 5 and 6in 3x3, summing 15
    • 11, 12, 13, 14 and 15 in 5x5 summing 65

Having this starting point, I took the task of solving the 7 by 7 square.

4 35 10 41 16 47 22
29 11 42 17 48 23 5
12 36 18 49 24 6 30
37 19 43 25 7 31 13
20 44 26 1 32 14 38
45 27 2 33 8 39 21
28 3 34 9 40 15 46

Where the sum of the numbers on every row column and both diagonals is 175.

Some more scrutiny and some more patterns become evident.
